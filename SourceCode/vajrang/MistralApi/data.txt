 1. Climate Differences: The USA and India have significantly different climates due to their geographical locations.
2. USA Seasons: In the USA, winter is the coldest season with snowfall in northern states, spring is a transitional season with warming temperatures and blooming flowers, summer is the hottest season with temperatures exceeding 90°F (32°C) in many areas, and fall is a beautiful season with leaves changing color and temperatures cooling down.
3. India Seasons: In India, winter is milder than in the USA with temperatures ranging from 50°F (10°C) to 77°F (25°C), pre-monsoon is the hottest season with temperatures often exceeding 100°F (38°C), monsoon is the rainy season bringing relief from heat and flooding in some areas, and post-monsoon sees a gradual cooling down of temperatures and drying up of monsoon rains, along with the harvest festival season.
4. USA Winter: Winter brings heavy snowfall to northern states such as New York, Michigan, and Minnesota, with temperatures dropping below freezing in many areas.
5. India Winter: Indian winters are milder than those in the USA, but some regions experience heavy snowfall, particularly in Jammu and Kashmir. Temperatures range from 50°F (10°C) to 77°F (25°C).
